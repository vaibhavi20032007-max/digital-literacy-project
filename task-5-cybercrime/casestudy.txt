Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud
UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime in India where fraudsters trick users into sending money through digital payment apps. This type of fraud usually begins with a scammer contacting the victim through a phone call, message, or social media platform.
In a typical scenario, the fraudster pretends to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. They send a fake payment request or QR code and ask the victim to scan it to “receive” money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly approves a payment and loses funds.
Students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems are often targeted. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes leakage of personal banking details.
This case highlights the importance of understanding how UPI transactions work. Users should always remember that money is never received by scanning a QR code. Awareness and careful verification are key to preventing such frauds
